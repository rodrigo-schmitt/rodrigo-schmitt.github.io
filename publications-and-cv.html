    • *For non-Portuguese speakers:
    Space technologies applied to urban planning
    This work aims to relate society, space technologies and environment through the case study of Brazilian environmental issues, oriented by satellite remote sensing. This study, which started in the Winter School of Space Technologies of 2019 (CIITE 2019) from the National Institute of Space Research (INPE), integrated the above topics with the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) from the United Nations. The use of space technologies, then, was chosen as the method of analysing environmental problems in five Brazilian cities, representing each country region and their characteristic problems, in order to explore urban planning in a sustainable and smart way. Through the use of remote sensing tools and the Geographical Information System (GIS), it was possible to characterize the cities with respect to their chosen problems, propose practical solutions of urban planning and develop an Integrated System of Environment and Urban Development (SIMADU) as a means to unify techniques of urban planning analysis using satellite technology.
